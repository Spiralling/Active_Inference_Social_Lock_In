% =====================================================================
\section{Multi-Agent Model}
\label{sec:model}
% =====================================================================

We now lift the single-agent apparatus of Section~\ref{sec:formal} to a
coupled population whose dynamics close the two loops of
Figure~\ref{fig:coupled-loop}. The model has four moving parts: a
\emph{structured} population on a network (Section~\ref{ssec:setup});
an active-inference policy over experiment choice whose objective is
the agent's expected resource gain (Section~\ref{ssec:policy}); a
$\stub$-modified posterior update that incorporates the
socially-induced utility of Eq.~\eqref{eq:utility}
(Section~\ref{ssec:update}); and the per-step dynamics that compose
private inference, trust-weighted pooling, the Gamma-conjugate trust
update, and the trust-driven resource update
(Section~\ref{ssec:dynamics}). Section~\ref{ssec:path-dependence}
states the path-dependence claim the experimental programme of
Section~\ref{sec:experiments} targets.

% --------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{A Structured Population on a Network}
\label{ssec:setup}
% --------------------------------------------------------------------

The population is a set $\agentset = \{1, \dots, \Nagents\}$ of agents
on a social graph $\graph = (\agentset, \edgeset)$. The graph is
\emph{exogenous} and structured: $\agentset$ partitions into
communities $\agentset = \community{1} \cup \dots \cup \community{K}$
--- factions, schools, a vanguard and a mainstream --- that differ in
their initial posteriors or their traits. We treat this structure as a
first-class object, for a substantive reason: a \emph{homogeneous}
population on a symmetric graph is a degenerate case in which
trust-weighted pooling collapses to a uniform neighbour average, every
agent tracks the same trajectory, and the interesting dynamics ---
contested periods, asymmetric conversion, paradigm capture --- are
invisible to a population-mean description (Remark~\ref{rem:no-pool}).
We therefore report per-community and per-agent quantities throughout,
and never a single population mean.

Each agent $i$ carries three pieces of state:
\begin{itemize}
\item a Gaussian posterior over the world parameter,
  $\Q_i(\thetapar) = \mathcal{N}(\muest_i, \tauprec_i^{-1})$, with mean
  $\muest_i$ and precision $\tauprec_i$ (Section~\ref{ssec:inference});
\item a row of the trust matrix --- the Gamma hyperparameters
  $(\shapegam_{ij}, \rategam_{ij})$ and induced precisions
  $\trust{i}{j}$ for $j \in \nbhd{i} \cup \{i\}$, including the
  self-trust $\trust{i}{i}$ (Section~\ref{ssec:trust});
\item a scalar resource state $\resource{i} \in \Real_{\geq 0}$
  (Section~\ref{ssec:resource}). The patronage matrix $\flowmat$ is
  \emph{not} per-agent state: it is the row-normalised trust matrix
  (Eq.~\ref{eq:flow-from-trust}) and is recomputed each step.
\end{itemize}
The cost of mind-change $\mindcost{i}$ is a per-agent cognitive trait
(fixed or slowly-varying) scaling the socially-induced utility
$\utility$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:utility}; it is not learned. The
environment supplies a truth $\thetastar(t)$ that may change over time;
Section~\ref{sec:experiments} specifies the schedules (a single step,
a ramp, and a reversal $A \!\to\! B \!\to\! A$).

% --------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The Active-Inference Policy: Expected Resource Gain}
\label{ssec:policy}
% --------------------------------------------------------------------

Each agent chooses \emph{which experiment to run} --- a value
$\xexp_i$ on a discrete grid --- to maximise its expected change in
resources,
\begin{equation}
\policy_i(\xexp)
\;\propto\;
\exp\!\Bigl(\betaexp\,\E\!\bigl[\Delta\resource{i} \mid \xexp_i = \xexp\bigr]\Bigr),
\label{eq:policy}
\end{equation}
with $\betaexp$ an inverse temperature controlling action exploration.
There is no separately-added information-gain term, no separately-added
strategic-gain term, and no separately-added experiment cost on top of
the policy objective: \emph{all of these enter through}
$\E[\Delta\resource{i}\!\mid\!\xexp]$, which decomposes (by the
resource update of Eq.~\ref{eq:resource-update} and the trust update
of Eq.~\ref{eq:trust-update}) as
\begin{equation}
\E\!\bigl[\Delta\resource{i} \mid \xexp\bigr]
\;\approx\;
\underbrace{\outflow\,\E\!\Bigl[\textstyle\sum_j \flow{j}{i}(t{+}1)\,\resource{j}(t) \mid \xexp\Bigr]}_{\substack{\text{trust-weighted inflow}\\\text{from neighbours}}}
\;+\;
\underbrace{\inflowrate\,\E\!\bigl[\inflowshare{i}(t{+}1) \mid \xexp\bigr]}_{\substack{\text{aggregate-trust}\\\text{exogenous share}}}
\;-\;
\underbrace{\gatecost(\xexp;\resource{i})}_{\substack{\text{cost of}\\\text{apparatus}}}
\;-\;
\rdecay\,\resource{i}(t).
\label{eq:expected-resource-gain}
\end{equation}
The two trust-mediated terms are what tie information to resources:
both depend on the post-observation trust
$\trust{j}{i}(t{+}1)$, which by Eq.~\eqref{eq:trust-update} responds
to the cross-surprisal $\surprisal_{ji}$ that neighbour $j$ records on
$i$'s predictive. In the \emph{paradigm-consistent regime} (low Fisher
information, Eq.~\ref{eq:fisher}), all agents' predictives are equally
close to the observation, the cross-surprisals do not differentiate
agents, no trust is reshuffled, and the expected resource gain is
dominated by the $-\gatecost - \rdecay\resource{i}$ floor: the agent
has no resource-route reason to run the experiment. In the
\emph{discriminating regime}, agents whose $\muest_i$ tracks
$\thetastar$ produce predictives that outperform their neighbours',
their incoming trust rises, and through
Eqs.~\eqref{eq:flow-from-trust}--\eqref{eq:inflow-share} their share
of both internal flow and exogenous inflow rises with it. Information
gain is therefore not a separate intrinsic value in the policy, as
in standard expected free energy; it is the \emph{instrumental}
channel by which the agent earns resources, viz., by being right where
being right is detectable.

The cost term $\gatecost(\xexp;\resource{i}) =
c_{0}\,\hone(\xexp)^{2}/\sigobs^{2}\cdot \phi(\resource{i})$ scales
with Fisher information --- discriminating experiments are expensive
in apparatus as well as informative --- with a barrier
$\phi(\resource{i}) = 1/\max(\resource{i} - r_{\min}, \epsilon)$ that
diverges as $\resource{i}$ falls below the cost of a discriminating
experiment. Axis~C of Section~\ref{sec:experiments} probes the
parameters of $\gatecost$.

\subsection{The $\stub$-Modified Posterior Update}
\label{ssec:update}
% --------------------------------------------------------------------

The per-agent update is the standard Gaussian filter of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:private-update}, modified by the $\stub$-scaled
socially-induced utility $\utility$ of Eq.~\eqref{eq:utility}. The
free-energy functional the agent minimises is
\begin{equation}
\Q_i^{\,\mathrm{priv}}
\;=\;
\arg\min_{\Q}
\Bigl\{
  \FE_i[\Q]
  \;-\;
  \mindcost{i}\,\utility\bigl(\Q;\,\{\Q_j(t), \resource{j}(t)\}_j\bigr)
\Bigr\},
\label{eq:lambda-update}
\end{equation}
where $\FE_i[\Q]$ is the standard variational free energy of $\Q$
against the agent's generative model and the new observation
$(\xexp_i, \obs_i)$. With $\utility$ as in Eq.~\eqref{eq:utility} and
the Gaussian family, Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambda-update} is again a
conjugate-Gaussian filter: the minimiser is Gaussian, with precision
$\tauprec_i^{\,\mathrm{priv}} = \tauprec_i^{\,\mathrm{data}} +
\mindcost{i}\sum_{j} (\resource{j}/\!\sum_k\!\resource{k})\,\tauprec_j$
and mean a precision-weighted combination of the data-driven posterior
(Eq.~\ref{eq:private-update}) with a resource-weighted neighbour mean.
The posterior is pulled toward the beliefs of high-resource
neighbours, with strength controlled by the agent's own $\mindcost{i}$;
the standard data-driven update is recovered at $\mindcost{i} = 0$.
Because $\resource{j}$ is itself a trust readout, the
$\stub$-pull effectively channels the same trust signal that drives
resource flow into the agent's own belief update, at a strength the
agent's $\stub$ controls.

\subsection{Per-Step Dynamics}
\label{ssec:dynamics}
% --------------------------------------------------------------------

One round composes the policy, the $\stub$-modified private update,
the Gamma-conjugate trust update, a trust-weighted pool, and the
trust-driven resource update. The trust-weighted pool over the closed
neighbourhood is unchanged from the standard multi-agent
active-inference treatment:
\begin{equation}
\Q_i^{(t+1)}(\thetapar)
\;\propto\;
\prod_{j \in \nbhd{i} \cup \{i\}}
  \bigl[\Q_j^{\,\mathrm{priv}}(\thetapar)\bigr]^{w_{ij}},
\qquad
w_{ij}
\;=\;
\frac{\trust{i}{j}}{\sum_{k \in \nbhd{i} \cup \{i\}} \trust{i}{k}}.
\label{eq:pool}
\end{equation}
For Gaussian posteriors the pool is closed-form: precisions add
weightedly and means precision-average. Algorithm~\ref{alg:step}
states the order of operations and makes the two coupled feedback
loops of Figure~\ref{fig:coupled-loop} explicit.

\begin{algorithm}
\caption{One step of the coupled belief--resource model.}
\label{alg:step}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State Environment sets the truth $\thetastar(t)$ for this round.
\For{each agent $i$}
  \State Choose experiment $\xexp_i \sim \policy_i$ via Eq.~\eqref{eq:policy}
         \Comment{policy = expected resource gain}
  \State Observe $\obs_i \sim \mathcal{N}\bigl(\hzero(\xexp_i)
         + \thetastar(t)\,\hone(\xexp_i),\, \sigobs^{2}\bigr)$.
  \State Form private posterior $\Q_i^{\,\mathrm{priv}}$ via
         Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambda-update}
         \Comment{$\stub$-scaled $\utility$ enters here}
\EndFor
\For{each agent $i$}
  \State Compute cross-surprisals $\surprisal_{ij}$ via
         Eq.~\eqref{eq:surprisal}.
  \State Update $(\shapegam_{ij}, \rategam_{ij}, \trust{i}{j})$ via
         Eq.~\eqref{eq:trust-update}.
  \State Pool $\{\Q_j^{\,\mathrm{priv}}\}_{j \in \nbhd{i} \cup \{i\}}$
         into $\Q_i^{(t+1)}$ via Eq.~\eqref{eq:pool}
         \Comment{\textbf{L2 closes}}
\EndFor
\For{each agent $i$}
  \State Read internal flow $\flow{j}{i}$ from $\trustmat$ via
         Eq.~\eqref{eq:flow-from-trust}.
  \State Read inflow share $\inflowshare{i}$ from $\trustmat$ via
         Eq.~\eqref{eq:inflow-share}.
  \State Resource update via Eq.~\eqref{eq:resource-update}
         \Comment{\textbf{L3 closes}}
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Path-Dependence Without a Pooled Trajectory}
\label{ssec:path-dependence}
% --------------------------------------------------------------------

The phenomenon the model is built to exhibit is \emph{paradigm
persistence}: the trajectory of the community's beliefs depends on the
order of events, not just their aggregate. In the coupled model the
substrate-level mechanism is \emph{trust-topology drift}: a forward
leg of an environmental schedule installs a particular distribution
$\{\resource{i}\}$ --- read off the trust matrix $\trustmat$ via
Eqs.~\eqref{eq:flow-from-trust}--\eqref{eq:inflow-share} --- that the
reverse leg does not retrace, because the trust update
(Eq.~\ref{eq:trust-update}) is sensitive to the entire history of
cross-surprisals rather than to the environmental schedule alone.
The three contributors picked apart in earlier informational
treatments --- trust drift, coupling lag, revision cost --- are the
downstream \emph{signatures} of this one substrate-level source: trust
drift is the substrate itself; coupling lag is the low-pass response
of the pool step; and the cost of mind-change is the cognitive
component of the same resource gradient that the trust topology
projects.

\begin{remark}[Why not a population mean]
\label{rem:no-pool}
It is tempting to summarise the community by a single
$(\thetastar,\bar\muest)$ trajectory. We deliberately do not: in a
homogeneous population the pool~\eqref{eq:pool} makes every agent
track the same trajectory and a population mean is adequate, but
precisely in that regime there is no contested period, no faction,
and nothing the structure does. Where the structure matters --- a
vanguard that converts first, a mainstream held back by a resource
bottleneck --- the population mean averages the phenomenon away. We
therefore state path-dependence over \emph{per-community} trajectories
and over \emph{resource-weighted} population summaries.
\end{remark}

\begin{claim}[Trust-driven path-dependence]
\label{claim:path-dependence}
Consider a structured population with at least two communities differing
in their initial posteriors, run under a reversal schedule
$\thetastar: A \to B \to A$. Under the full coupled dynamics of
Algorithm~\ref{alg:step}, the per-community trajectory
$\{(\thetastar(t),\, \bar\muest_{\community{k}}(t),\,
   G_{\community{k}}(t))\}$ ---
where $G_{\community{k}}$ is the within-community resource Gini ---
encloses a loop of positive area in the
$(\thetastar, \bar\muest_{\community{k}})$ plane. The loop area is
non-zero even with $\stub = 0$, because the trust matrix $\trustmat$
drifts under the schedule and the resulting resource topology gates
which experiments each community can run on the reverse leg via the
expected-resource-gain term of the policy
(Eq.~\ref{eq:expected-resource-gain}) and the apparatus-cost term
$\gatecost(\xexp;\resource{i})$. The loop area vanishes only when the
coupling from resources back into the policy is switched off
($\gatecost$ independent of $\resource{i}$) and the population is
homogeneous (so trust-topology drift produces no per-community
asymmetry).
\end{claim}

\noindent Claim~\ref{claim:path-dependence} is the target of the
experimental programme of Section~\ref{sec:experiments}; the three
axes there isolate the contributions of the initial trust topology
(Axis~A), of the centralisation of inflow induced by initial trust
concentration and the total inflow magnitude (Axis~B), and of the
experiment-cost--Fisher coupling (Axis~C).
