\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
% =====================================================================

Three immediate neighbours frame the contribution.

\paragraph{Single-agent cost of mind-change.}
\citet{hylandAlbarracin2025MindChange} re-foreground the complexity
term of variational free energy as a motivational quantity rather than
a regulariser: the agent experiences a cost proportional to the
Kullback--Leibler divergence between a candidate posterior and the
current prior. The asymmetry of that divergence implies that belief
revision is path-dependent --- the cost of reaching $C'$ from $C$ is
not the cost of reaching $C$ from $C'$. We adopt this reading and
extend it to the multi-agent setting, treating $\stub_{i,r}$ as a
per-agent, per-norm parameter that gates each agent's update to its
own salience priors.

\paragraph{Multi-agent active inference.}
\citet{albarracin2022EpistemicCommunities} establish the formal
apparatus for coupling pymdp agents through shared observations and
characterise echo-chamber bifurcation as a function of inter-agent
precision; \citet{constant2019Regimes} formalise norm-following as
Bayes-optimal action under environmentally cued policy priors;
\citet{albarracin2025SocialPower} formalise social power as the
differential capacity to set policy precision in others' generative
models. Our model inherits the coupling apparatus of
\citet{albarracin2022EpistemicCommunities} and the deontic-cue
mechanism of \citet{constant2019Regimes}, and complements
\citet{albarracin2025SocialPower} by operating one layer up: we treat
the \emph{content} of prior preferences ($\C$) rather than
policy-precision as the locus of normative dynamics.

\paragraph{Bayesian rule-induction baseline.}
\citet{oldenburgZhiXuan2024BayesianRule} treat norms as discrete
obligative or prohibitive rules induced from compliance and sanction
observations via Bayes' rule. Their inference is symmetric in time:
the same evidence accrual that locks in a rule will, when reversed,
unlock it on a comparable timescale. They have no asymmetric
revision cost. The $\stub \to 0$ limit of our model recovers
symmetric tracking; for $\stub > 0$, forward and reverse adaptation
trajectories diverge. This is the dissociation our experimental
programme (Section~\ref{sec:experiments}) targets directly.
